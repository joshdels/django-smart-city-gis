Where is your organization today?

Find the stage that best matches your land information workflow.

  1. Paper Records

    Maps, folders, and documents are managed manually.

  2. CAD & Survey

    AutoCAD drawings, survey plans, and technical descriptions are the primary source of information.

  3. Desktop GIS

    GIS software is used, but data is stored on individual computers.

  4. Department GIS

    One team or department maintains its own GIS data and workflows.

  5. Shared GIS

    A centralized GIS database is shared across multiple users.

  6. Web GIS

    Maps and parcel information are accessible through a web browser.

  7. Enterprise GIS

    Planning, Assessor, Engineering, and other departments work from the same GIS platform.

  8. Smart Land Management

    Automated workflows, dashboards, analytics, and digital decision-making.
